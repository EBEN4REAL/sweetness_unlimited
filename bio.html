About Abigail

Bio

Adeola Abigail Oridupa, also known by her stage name Abigail and credited in films as Abigail Oridupa, is a singer, actor, screenwriter, songwriter, production coordinator, film producer and entrepreneur. Upon her graduation from the Film and Theatre Academy where she was awarded the best graduating student in 2012, she founded Abdap International Concept, a full service audio-visual content provider and general merchandise company duly registered in Nigeria

Early Life

Now based in Lagos, Nigeria, Abigail spent the early years of her life growing up with a family of six, including her parents and three younger siblings in Ondo State, Southwest Nigeria.

Becoming a gynaecologist was Abigail’s childhood dream, not out of passion for medicine but because she thought parents determine their children’s future career since her father was fond of saying; “Adeola is very brilliant; she will grow up to be a medical doctor” In a recent interview, Abigail said; “My plan was to start following Daddy to the hospital after my SSCE (before I gain admission into the University to learn some basic elements of medicine from him, but unfortunately, death played a fast one on us by snatching him away on my first day in SS3.” .

At Sixteen, She was a scriptwriter for late veteran Broadcaster Ayo Omotosho on his radio programme LOVE ZONE. Long before then in primary school, Abigail wrote her first playlets (which she made into small booklets with sheets of papers bound with broomsticks) for her friends to read. She composed her first song at six “Ice water o Ice water Gbogbo School in community o Ice water”

“I think what I was trying to say then is: All the schools in Okeluse, my home town in Ose Local Government Area of Ondo State, deserved some chilled water (laughs)”.

Lifestyle

Abigail is a positive thinker, so you will often hear her say “We will be finer than this” one of her happiness bug is watching people dancing and smiling and that has inspired most of her music composition.

“I’ll be doing more of the afro-soul and afro-pop genre. As you know, my first single, ELDORADO, is a kind of song that gladdens the heart and make you shake your body; that’s exactly what I like to see people do.”

Abigail’s core values are: Integrity, simplicity and excellence. The trails of these can be seen in both her private life and career. She is a trooper who believes in teamwork, ever ready to learn and impact lives positively with what she knows. She detests lying and dirtiness.

Some of her favorite people in no particular order are: Jenifer Lopez, Genevive Nnaji, Omawumi Magbele, Serita Ann Jakes, Folorunso Alakija, Chris Ihidero, Samuel Eto'o, Akon, Richard Branson and Barrack Obama.

Abigail would rather go to a house full of demons than sleep in a room with cockroach. Why? “I believe at the name of Jesus, every demon will vanish back to hell, but for cockroaches, I will have to kill it and they stink.

Journeying On

With over one hundred and one songs and numerous stories written across different genres, Abigail Oridupa believes there is an appointed time for every story to be told and she hopes to tell most of these stories, if not all one at a time via music, films and books, before she bids earth goodbye.
